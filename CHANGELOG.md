### Added
- `docs/MDE_TABLE.md` + `scripts/calibrate_mde.py` + `tests/test_mde_consistency.py` — premortem-mitigation #4 pre-sweep methodological check. The doc tabulates Wilson 95% CI half-widths at $p \in \{0.02, 0.05, 0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 0.90, 0.95, 0.98\}$ for $N \in \{50, 100, 250, 500\}$ (the contracted N=250 plus the auto-downscope variants), the per-cell "inconclusive band" $2 \cdot \text{HW}$ scaling with $\hat{p}$, the closed-form independence MDE bound $(z_{0.975}+z_{0.8}) \sqrt{2p(1-p)/N}$ at $\rho \in \{0, 0.3, 0.5\}$, and the empirical paired bootstrap MDE from a $9 \times 3 \times 6$ $(p, \rho, \Delta)$ grid simulated at 1000 outer iterations × 2000 bootstrap resamples per cell under `numpy.random.default_rng(seed=42)`. Headline numbers: Wilson half-width at $p=0.5, N=250$ is **0.0615**; the inconclusive band $2 \cdot \text{HW}$ is **0.1230**; empirical paired MDE at $p=0.5, \rho=0$ is **0.15** and at $\rho=0.3$ is also **0.15** — both more conservative than the closed-form 0.125 because the percentile bootstrap pays a small width tax for not assuming normality. Practical interpretation: any (policy, env) pair whose observed $|\Delta\hat{p}|$ is below $2 \cdot \text{HW}(\max(\hat{p}_a, \hat{p}_b), N)$ must be displayed as "inconclusive at $N{=}250$" in the leaderboard, not as a ranked ordering. `scripts/calibrate_mde.py` reproduces the simulation grid (~5 min on a 2026 laptop CPU; `--pilot --n-outer 50` runs in ~15 s for CI); the script imports nothing from torch/lerobot/gymnasium and is AST-guarded by the test suite. 7 tests in `tests/test_mde_consistency.py` (Wilson HW at $p=0.5, N=250$ matches the doc to 4 dp, inconclusive band $2 \cdot \text{HW}$ matches, Wilson HW symmetric around $p=0.5$, simulated paired MDE at $(p=0.5, N=250, \rho=0, \text{seed}=42)$ in $[0.10, 0.20]$, doc § 1 table parsed by regex with each row's "inconclusive band" column = $2 \times$ "half-width" column AND each row's HW matches `wilson_ci` output, AST guard on `calibrate_mde.py` top-level imports, end-to-end smoke that `--pilot` exits 0).

### Changed
- `notebooks/01-write-finding.ipynb` — added cell 5b (markdown header + code) immediately after the paired-comparison cell. Cell 5b implements the **per-cell MDE gate** from `docs/MDE_TABLE.md` § 4: a hard-coded Wilson half-width table at $N=250$ (with a docstring pointer to the doc as source of truth), a `_per_cell_inconclusive_band(p_hat, n)` helper that snaps `p_hat` to the closest tabulated $p$ and rescales by $\sqrt{250/n}$ for auto-downscoped cells, and a derived `paired_df_gated` DataFrame with new columns `per_cell_inconclusive_band` + `inconclusive_per_cell` (Boolean = `|delta| < 2 * HW(max(rate_a, rate_b), n_pairs)`). The previous cell-5 `inconclusive_at_N` flag (which used the worst-case $p=0.5$ band uniformly) is preserved for back-compat; the new per-cell flag is the gate the leaderboard renderer should consume — flagged comparisons must render in neutral grey and be excluded from the abstract's headline-finding sentence. Notebook re-executed end-to-end via `nbclient.NotebookClient(timeout=120)` against the synthetic fallback parquet; both synthetic comparisons (delta = -0.008 on Aloha and -0.036 on PushT, both at $\hat{p} \approx 0.7$, band $\approx 0.107$) correctly flag as inconclusive — a useful sanity check that the gate fires when it should.
- `paper/main.tex` § Methods MDE-bound paragraph — replaced the rounded `≈ 0.062` / `≈ 0.124` with the precise computed values **0.0615** / **0.1230**, added the `wilson_ci(125, 250)` source-of-truth pointer plus a citation to `docs/MDE_TABLE.md` for the full $p$-grid + auto-downscope variants + empirical bootstrap MDE at $\rho \in \{0, 0.3, 0.5\}$. Updated `tab:paired` caption to call out that the inconclusive threshold is **per-cell** ($2 \cdot \text{HW}(\max(\hat{p}_A, \hat{p}_B), N)$, not a flat $0.124$); the worst case $\hat{p} = 0.5$ value is restated for the reader who does not chase the doc.
- `src/lerobot_bench/render.py` — `render_episode` now walks an adaptive `RENDER_LADDER` of `(fps, crf)` rungs (`(10,23) -> (5,23) -> (5,28) -> (5,33)`) until the encoded clip fits under `MAX_BYTES`, instead of single-shot encoding at fixed `crf=23` and erroring on overshoot. Premortem mitigation for real Aloha episodes (1000+ steps) blowing the 2 MiB cap. The successful rung index is recorded in the new `EncoderSettings.rung_index` field (`-1` when the caller passes explicit `fps`/`crf` to bypass the ladder; `>=1` is "playback faster than wall-clock"). Pathological inputs that overshoot every rung still raise `RenderSizeError`, now carrying the full per-rung attempt log in the message and a structured `attempts` tuple. We do *not* drop input frames — lower fps means faster playback, which is the documented tradeoff over sample loss.
- `docs/CEO-PLAN.md` — appended an "Infrastructure (added 2026-04-30)" section noting the agent team, hooks, CI evolution, and operational docs. Strategy unchanged.
