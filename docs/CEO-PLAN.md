## Premise reframing

**Original framing:** "Build a public LeRobot multi-policy benchmark" — treats the leaderboard as the goal.

**CEO reframing:** The benchmark is a proxy. The actual goal is **land the HF Robotics role**. A leaderboard alone signals "competent benchmarker" — necessary but not sufficient. The artifact is reframed as a triptych:

1. **Benchmark + leaderboard** (the spine — same as the design doc)
2. **Arxiv-grade writeup** (the soul — interprets the data, defends the finding, demonstrates researcher signal)
3. **Upstream PR to huggingface/lerobot** (the credential — extracts the eval pipeline as a clean module HF can adopt)

Each piece compounds with the others. The benchmark IS the data the writeup analyzes; the upstream PR IS the eval module that runs the benchmark; the writeup ends with "and here's the PR."

This is the same compute and roughly the same days as the original Approach B in the design doc, but with substantially more surface area on the dimensions HF reviewers actually care about: contributions to lerobot, demonstrated VLA understanding, paper-quality output, public artifacts.

## Accepted Scope (added to v1)

- **C1 — Triptych reframe**: artifact reframed from "leaderboard" to "leaderboard + writeup + PR." Updates the success criteria, distribution plan, and day schedule.
- **V1 — Fine-tune track**: pick the worst-performing (policy, env) cell from benchmark results. Fine-tune that policy with LoRA on 100 public demos from the lerobot Hub dataset for that env. Show before/after success rate with error bars. Document either improvement OR no-improvement honestly.
- **V2 — Arxiv-grade writeup**: a 4-page paper replacing the original notebook deliverable. Sections: Abstract, Methods (envs, policies, eval protocol, statistics), Results (leaderboard table + finding figure + failure taxonomy), Discussion (what the finding means, limitations, future work), References. LaTeX template. Submit to arxiv.
- **V3 — Distribution moment**: 90-second screen-record walkthrough of the Space. Twitter thread (4-6 tweets) tagging key HF Robotics members. Posts to HF Discord and HF Forum. Day-after-launch only — never before the artifact is fully ready.
- **D1 — Failure taxonomy**: manually categorize failure rollouts (sample 5-10 per cell) into modes: trajectory overshoot, gripper slip, timeout, wrong-object, premature release, drift. Renders as a horizontal bar chart per policy in the writeup.

## Temporal interrogation (decisions to lock NOW)

These will surface during implementation if not pre-decided:

1. **Fine-tune target**: which (policy, env) cell to fine-tune? Decision rule: lowest-success policy on Libero (the env where VLAs should shine). If Libero is dropped per the 4-hr spike cap, fall back to lowest-success on Aloha.
2. **LoRA rank for fine-tune**: 8 or 16. Decision: start at 16 unless OOM, then drop to 8.
3. **Demo dataset for fine-tune**: which HF Hub dataset for the chosen env? Decision: lock at end of Day 0a alongside policy IDs.
4. **arxiv category**: cs.RO (robotics) primary, cs.LG secondary. Decision: at Day 8.
5. **Twitter handle list**: which HF Robotics members to tag in the thread? Decision: by Day 9, after writeup is drafted.
6. **arxiv submission timing**: same day as Space launch, or 24 hrs after? Recommendation: same day, so the Twitter thread can include the arxiv link.
7. **What to do if fine-tune does NOT improve success rate?** Decision: document the negative result in the writeup as a finding ("fine-tuning with 100 demos was insufficient to lift policy X above baseline on env Y — likely because [reason]"). Negative results are still researcher signal; do not silently drop the fine-tune track.

## Risks (added by expansion)

- **Pi0 fine-tuning OOM**: even with LoRA, Pi0's 3B params may not fit fine-tuning on 8GB. Mitigation: choose SmolVLA for the fine-tune track if Pi0 is the worst cell; SmolVLA is smaller and fine-tunes more reliably on 8GB.
- **Writeup quality risk**: a half-baked arxiv paper is worse than a polished notebook. Mitigation: timebox writeup to 1.5 days, accept that v1 may be 4 pages of strong methods + 1 page of limitations; iterate post-launch.
- **Distribution backfire**: if the artifact has bugs at launch, a Twitter thread amplifies the bugs. Mitigation: distribution day is +24 hours from Space launch, not same-day.
